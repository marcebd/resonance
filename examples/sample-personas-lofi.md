# Sample Personas — Lo-Fi Hip Hop Panel

> **Brief:** Lo-fi hip hop, melancholic, 75 BPM, instrumental. For background study during rainy evenings. Audience: college students.

**Parsed demographic:** college students, 18-24

---

## Tomás, 19, Guadalajara

**Occupation:** First-year architecture student
**Favorite genres:** lo-fi hip hop, ambient electronic, bossa nova, city pop
**Listening contexts:** late-night drafting sessions with headphones on (treating music as a tool to block out roommates), walking between campus buildings before classes start
**Discovery:** YouTube rabbit holes from specific lo-fi compilations, asking classmates what they had playing in the studio during crits, Spotify Radio seeded from anchor tracks he replays obsessively

**Traits:**
- Treats lo-fi as functional infrastructure, not art — will abandon a track within 90 seconds if it demands his attention rather than receding into the background
- Unusually sensitive to loop fatigue: if a sample repeats with no variation after two minutes he notices it consciously and it breaks his focus
- Has no patience for rain or vinyl crackle as atmospheric devices — considers them clichéd shorthand and finds them condescending
- Se engancha emocionalmente con el piano más que con cualquier otro instrumento — un motivo melódico de piano bien colocado puede hacerlo guardar una canción de inmediato

## Yejin, 22, Seoul

**Occupation:** Fourth-year biochemistry student and part-time café barista
**Favorite genres:** lo-fi hip hop, Korean indie folk, post-rock, emo
**Listening contexts:** studying at the café counter during slow afternoon shifts (one earbud in), lying on her bed after midnight explicitly NOT studying — using music to process stress rather than ignore it
**Discovery:** curated Melon and Bugs playlists tagged with specific moods (cross-referenced with what coffeehouse regulars request), Twitter/X music community accounts that post obscure lo-fi releases, friend recommendations as voice messages with timestamps ("listen from 1:47")

**Traits:**
- Listens to lo-fi actively, not passively — expects melodic development or textural shift to reward sustained attention, and feels cheated by tracks that plateau early
- Strongly attuned to emotional authenticity; will disengage from a track that feels engineered for mood-board aesthetics rather than felt experience
- Reads tempo relative to her own anxiety level — on high-stress exam weeks, tracks at 75 BPM feel dragging rather than calming
- Collector mentality: keeps a running ranked list of lo-fi tracks by "replayability per emotion" and shares it with exactly three friends

## Dariusz, 21, Kraków

**Occupation:** Third-year computer science student
**Favorite genres:** vaporwave, lo-fi hip hop, IDM, dark ambient
**Listening contexts:** four-hour focused coding blocks at his desk (headphones as a signal to flatmates that he is unreachable), late evening bus rides home from the university library
**Discovery:** Rate Your Music / Sonemic deep dives sorting genres by average rating, YouTube channels dedicated to obscure vaporwave subgenres, Reddit r/LoFiHipHop and r/vaporwave prioritizing tracks with fewer than 10k plays

**Traits:**
- Driven almost entirely by production texture — will save a structurally empty track if the sample grain or reverb tail is unusual, will skip a melodically strong track if the mix sounds sterile
- Actively hostile to lo-fi that sounds algorithmically generated or templated; has developed an informal checklist of "tells" for AI-produced tracks and uses it consciously
- Needs a track to feel like it was made by someone with a specific point of view, not a genre exercise — generic chord progressions over a boom-bap beat register as filler regardless of execution quality
- Rarely shares music socially; his engagement metric is personal: does he add it to a specific local playlist folder or not

## Amara, 23, Accra

**Occupation:** Final-year communications student and freelance social media manager
**Favorite genres:** afrobeats, lo-fi hip hop, R&B, neo-soul
**Listening contexts:** writing essays in her bedroom with laptop speakers low (treating music as white noise with shape), shared study sessions with two close friends where what's playing is a group negotiation
**Discovery:** TikTok tracks that appear in multiple study-with-me videos from creators she follows, Spotify's Daylist (treats it as a starting point), WhatsApp group chats where her friend group shares links without context and expects a reaction within the hour

**Traits:**
- Reads music as social signal even in solo study contexts — will only keep playing a track if she could imagine recommending it to her study group without embarrassment
- Has a low tolerance for melancholy that tips into what she calls "grey" — a flatness of mood she associates with bad study sessions; needs even sad music to carry a faint warmth
- Highly responsive to bass texture: a well-placed sub-bass movement makes a track feel grounded, and its absence makes lo-fi feel thin and unfinished
- Tends to stop listening after two tracks if nothing has surprised her — she applies the same impatience to playlists that she applies to social feeds

## Lieselotte, 26, Utrecht

**Occupation:** Master's student in urban planning and part-time municipal policy intern
**Favorite genres:** ambient, modern classical, lo-fi hip hop, slowcore
**Listening contexts:** long focused reading sessions at her kitchen table on rainy afternoons (window open, music low), cycling to her internship — using music to mentally transition between student and professional modes
**Discovery:** Bandcamp new arrivals filtered by tag combinations refined over three years ("lo-fi", "melancholic", "instrumental"), The Wire's online reviews, recommendations from one specific friend whose taste she trusts completely

**Traits:**
- Loyal to artists over tracks — a release that sounds inconsistent with an artist's previous work feels like a breach of trust, which affects her rating of the track itself
- Has a graduate student's critical impatience with anything that aestheticizes difficulty or sadness without earning it; melancholy must feel specific, not decorative
- Prefers spatial production — tracks that use stereo width and room depth feel more "liveable" during long study sessions
- Completely indifferent to BPM as a category but acutely sensitive to rhythmic feel — a 75 BPM track can feel either meditative or sluggish depending entirely on how the drums sit in the mix

## Callum, 31, Edinburgh

**Occupation:** Secondary school music teacher (edge persona — older than target)
**Favorite genres:** jazz, post-bop, soul, lo-fi hip hop
**Listening contexts:** marking student work at his kitchen table on weekday evenings (needs music that doesn't compete with reading), passive background during dinner preparation
**Discovery:** NTS Radio and Worldwide FM late-night sets where lo-fi appears alongside jazz and soul, colleagues' casual mentions rather than active searching (discovers lo-fi sideways, not genre-first), occasionally follows a Spotify playlist a former student sent him two years ago

**Traits:**
- Evaluates lo-fi through a jazz literacy lens — chord voicings that are vague or lazy register as wrong even when functioning correctly as atmosphere; this reaction is involuntary
- Maintains a faint professional skepticism toward the genre's relationship to the jazz and soul it samples
- Responds well to tracks with a clear sense of restraint — notes or sounds that feel deliberately withheld are more interesting than those that fill space
- Has almost no patience for music that announces its own nostalgia; tape hiss and vinyl noise read to him as costume rather than feeling
