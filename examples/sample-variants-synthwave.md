# Sample Variants — Dark Synthwave

> **Brief:** Dark synthwave instrumental, 100 BPM, retro 80s sci-fi vibes. For nighttime driving playlists.

**Parsed attributes:**
- Genre: dark synthwave
- Mood: cinematic, brooding
- Tempo: 100 BPM
- Use case: nighttime driving playlist
- Demographic: 25-40 synthwave enthusiasts and retro sci-fi fans

---

## Variant A — THE NEON OVERPASS

**Hook:** A three-oscillator arpeggio locked to a gated snare grid that drives the entire arrangement without ever resolving — tension held for four full minutes.

**Description:** The track that owns the stretch of highway between the last streetlight and the city glow — pure Carpenter Brut production architecture with Perturbator's melodic sadism, arpeggiated lead cutting through the mix like headlights through fog. Gated reverb snare hits on every 2 and 4 like a pulse monitor in a sci-fi med bay, and the bassline never lets up.

**Production:** Juno-106 pads, Moog bassline, OB-Xd arpeggiated lead, TR-808 with gated reverb, analog delay side-chain. Structure: cold open - A section - B section - drop - A section - outro fadeout. Tempo: 100 BPM. Key: F minor.

---

## Variant B — THE DEAD SATELLITE

**Hook:** Minimalist call-and-response between a single Rhodes-style electric piano lead and sparse, wide-panned pad swells — most of the impact comes from what's pulled out of the mix, not what's in it.

**Description:** This one hits when you're 45 minutes into the drive and the radio cuts out — scored like a John Carpenter cue that never made the film, pure dread under a slow-burn melodic surface. Minimal, almost empty in the low end, which makes the one synth chord stab at the two-minute mark land like a gut punch in a dark theater.

**Production:** Rhodes-emulated keys (Keyscape), hollow sub bass, wide stereo pad layers, very sparse TR-909 hi-hats, tape reverb send. Structure: intro - sparse verse - build - chord stab climax - decay outro. Tempo: 100 BPM (half-time feel in verse). Key: D minor.

---

## Variant C — THE CHROME CORRIDOR

**Hook:** Stacked four-layer synth lead with detuned unison doubling, riding over a relentless sequenced bassline and a full live-drum-meets-drum-machine hybrid kit — maximum density, maximum forward motion.

**Description:** Full maximalist grid — every frequency slot occupied, the kind of track that soundtracked a chase scene in a movie that went straight to VHS in 1987 and became a cult object. Tangerine Dream's density married to Gunship's modern mix polish, with a lead synth melody that's almost too melodic, too hopeful, which makes the darkness underneath hit harder.

**Production:** Stacked Minimoog lead (4 layers, detuned), Prophet-5 chords, sequenced Juno bass, hybrid drum kit (live snare + LinnDrum), orchestral brass stabs via sample. Structure: intro - full build - chorus A - breakdown - chorus B (layered) - outro. Tempo: 100 BPM. Key: B♭ minor.

---

## Variant D — THE PARKING STRUCTURE

**Hook:** A four-note sequenced motif on a detuned lead that shifts modal center every 32 bars without the rhythm section moving — creates disorientation over a locked groove.

**Description:** Not the highway — the concrete spiral down to level B3, fluorescent flicker, no other cars. Cluster Mk. II industrialism crossed with S U R V I V E's acid-bath atmosphere, a drone-heavy floor with a brutalist four-note motif that repeats and mutates across six minutes like something being assembled in the dark. Zero resolution.

**Production:** ARP 2600 modular lead, acid 303 bassline, industrial textured pads (granular processed), TR-606 stripped-down kick-snare pattern, heavy sidechain compression on pads. Structure: drone intro - motif entry - variation 1 - variation 2 - stripped breakdown - motif reprise - hard cut. Tempo: 100 BPM. Key: G# minor (modal shifts to Phrygian in variation 2).

---

## Variant E — THE LAST EXIT RAMP

**Hook:** A single four-bar synth melody stated clean, then restated with a full orchestral pad swell and delay-drenched harmony layer — the arrangement's entire emotional weight loaded into that one return.

**Description:** 4am, the drive is ending, and there's a specific melancholy to turning off the engine — Vangelis Blade Runner elegance with Makeup and Vanity Set's narrative arc, a slow-motion melodic statement that builds into something that almost qualifies as beautiful before the filter closes it down. The one on the playlist people screenshot and Shazam the next morning.

**Production:** Solo DX7-style FM lead, Oberheim pad swell, fretless bass (sampled), orchestral string section (BBCSO lite), subtle TR-707 rim clicks only. Structure: solo intro - verse A - verse B - orchestral swell climax - solo reprise (filtered down) - silence. Tempo: 100 BPM (rubato feel on lead). Key: E minor.
