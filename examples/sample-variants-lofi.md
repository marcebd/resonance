## Variant A — THE LIBRARY CLOSE

**Hook:** A two-bar Rhodes loop with deliberate pitch drift on the tail-end chord, making the progression feel perpetually unresolved.

**Description:** The track that plays when the fluorescent lights go off section by section and you're the last one packing up. Vintage Rhodes chords chopped on the 2 and 4, Knxwledge-style micro-cuts with J Dilla's pocket — the beat breathes wrong on purpose, and that wrongness is the whole point.

**Production:** Rhodes piano (chopped), upright bass (plucked, low-mixed), dusty boom-bap drum breaks, vinyl crackle as constant layer. Structure: intro (4 bars) - main loop A (8 bars) - main loop B with bass swell (8 bars) - breakdown (4 bars) - main loop A return - outro fade. Tempo: 75 BPM. Key: Eb minor.

## Variant B — THE WINDOW SEAT

**Hook:** A muted trumpet phrase appears at the midpoint as a single-occurrence counter-melody, then disappears — creating a memory the listener chases for the rest of the track.

**Description:** You've had this tab open for 45 minutes and read the same paragraph three times — this is the track underneath that. Nujabes-era jazz-forward palette meets Idealism's textural warmth: live brushed snare, a muted trumpet counter-melody that surfaces only once and never returns.

**Production:** Muted trumpet (live-recorded, lo-fi processed), brushed snare kit, upright bass, Wurlitzer chords, light rain field recording woven into mix. Structure: intro (8 bars) - verse loop (16 bars) - mid breakdown with trumpet appearance (8 bars) - return loop (16 bars) - outro (8 bars, rain rises). Tempo: 74 BPM. Key: D minor.

## Variant C — THE EMPTY KITCHEN

**Hook:** Negative space engineered as a structural element — every 4th bar drops to a single sustained guitar note and bare kick, no fills, no resolution.

**Description:** 3am, everyone else asleep, you're grinding through a paper with the overhead light off and just the stove hood light on — this track IS that light. Minimal to the point of ache: single-note guitar picking over a boom-bap shell, negative space so wide the silences stack up like unread messages, closer to Ichiko Aoba's stillness than anything Spotify tags lo-fi.

**Production:** Nylon-string acoustic guitar (close-mic'd, room noise audible), kick and snare only (no hi-hats), sub-bass drone (sustained, barely present), ambient room tone. Structure: intro (4 bars, guitar only) - loop A (8 bars) - loop A + bass drone (8 bars) - total strip (4 bars) - loop A return (8 bars) - fade. Tempo: 75 BPM. Key: A minor.

## Variant D — THE BUS RIDE BACK

**Hook:** Three detuned harmonic layers — Rhodes, a flute sample, and a reversed guitar chord — stacked so they phase in and out of alignment across every 8-bar cycle.

**Description:** Headphones in on the late bus home, streetlights strobing through rain-streaked glass, the city outside muffled to a hum — this track builds that bubble around you. Maximalist lo-fi: MF DOOM's layered sample density meets Clams Casino's atmospheric fog, three simultaneous chord sources all slightly detuned from each other, vinyl crackle replaced by cassette tape hiss that sits hot in the high-mids.

**Production:** Rhodes (detuned +12 cents), flute sample (chopped, reversed tail), reversed electric guitar chord, trap hi-hat pattern at half-time, 808 sub kick, cassette hiss (high-mid present). Structure: intro (4 bars) - build loop A (8 bars) - build loop B + flute layer in (8 bars) - full density drop (16 bars) - breakdown (8 bars) - outro swell (8 bars). Tempo: 75 BPM. Key: F# minor.

## Variant E — THE SECOND COFFEE

**Hook:** A pentatonic koto sample is chopped and quantized against a swing-heavy boom-bap grid, creating rhythmic tension between the instrument's natural decay and the beat's pocket.

**Description:** Mid-afternoon hits a wall, you pour another cup you don't need, and just sit there for a moment before opening the next tab — this track owns that pause. Tatsuro Yamashita's warmth filtered through East Asian lo-fi (Mondo Loops era), pentatonic koto sample chopped into a boom-bap grid, chord progression that never fully lands on the tonic so the whole track hovers.

**Production:** Koto sample (chopped, swing-quantized), warm upright bass, boom-bap drum break with heavy swing (65% swing factor), soft electric piano pad (long release, low velocity), cassette saturation on master bus. Structure: intro (koto only, 4 bars) - main loop (16 bars) - piano pad layer enters (8 bars) - main loop return (16 bars) - outro fade with koto only (4 bars). Tempo: 75 BPM. Key: G pentatonic minor.
